Som Tum

Som Tum is a traditional Thai salad made of green papaya. Commonly served by street vendors across the country, there are a number of different ways this dish can be prepared depending on the region.

This recipe is a relatively standard som tum inspired by the street vendors I frequented while living in Thailand.

Equipment

  • A large mortar and pestle (kruk) like the one linked here
  • Chef's knife
  • Skillet (stainless steel, non-stick or cast iron all work)
  • Cutting board
  • Wooden spoon to mix
  • Julienne peeler

Ingredients

  • 1x Green papaya
  • 2 - 3 tbsps of fish sauce
  • 3x fresh limes to juice
  • Bird's eye chilies (as little as 0 as many as you want)
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 5-7 cherry tomatoes or 1 roma tomato
  • 1 stalk of long bean (or 3-4 green beans)
  • 2 tbsps unsalted, blanched red or white peanutes to toast
  • 1 tbsp Dried shrimp
  • 1-2 tbsps Palm sugar (white sugar will work in it's place)

Steps

  1. Toast peanuts in a heated skillet then crush them in the mortar and pestle. Set aside
  2. Peel and julienne (using the peeler) your green papaya. You'll want to have at least 2 handfuls (3 ish cups) to make the salad with. Once julienned, set the papaya aside in a bowl with cold water
  3. Juice limes
  4. Chop green beans into 6cm pieces. If using a roma tomato, chop this into 8 pieces
  5. Combine garlic and chilies in mortar and crush until a paste is formed. Depending on how hot you like it, you can add anywhere from 1 to 5 chilies (or more, you do you)
  6. Add palm sugar to mortar and crush until blended
  7. Add papaya and crush using the spoon to help move the papaya around in between. The goal here isn't to pulverize the papaya, just soften it a bit
  8. Add lime juice and fish sauce contining to mix and crush
  9. Add dried shrimp, toasted peanuts, tomatoes and beans mixing well
  10. Plate and serve with sticky rice

Pad Krapow

Home

Transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Thailand with Pad Krapow Gai, a stir-fry that perfectly captures the essence of Thai cuisine. This dish is a marvel of balance, showcasing the holy trinity of Thai flavors: spicy, sweet, and savory. Pad Krapow Gai, or Thai Basil Chicken, is a fast-paced dance of tender chicken slices wok-tossed with the fire of chilies and the aromatic punch of holy basil. This dish is a staple in Thai eateries and a beloved quick meal for locals, often served over a bed of steamed jasmine rice and crowned with a glistening fried egg.

Equipment

  • Wok or large frying pan
  • Wooden spoon or spatula
  • Knife and cutting board
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Mortar and pestle (optional, for making chili paste)
  • Small bowl (for mixing sauces)
  • Saucepan (if making rice)
  • Serving plate

Ingredients

  • Ingredient 1
  • 1 lb (450g) chicken breast or thigh, finely chopped or ground
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely minced
  • 2-3 bird's eye chilies, finely chopped (adjust to heat preference)
  • 2 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced (optional)
  • 2 cups of holy basil leaves, loosely packed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 teaspoons light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce (for color, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth or water
  • Fried eggs (one per serving, optional)

Steps

  1. Prep Work
    • Begin by prepping all your ingredients. Finely chop or grind the chicken if not already done. Mince the garlic and chilies finely (you can use a mortar and pestle if you have one for a more traditional approach). Thinly slice the shallots and red bell pepper if using.
    • In a small bowl, combine the oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Stir well until the sugar is mostly dissolved.
  2. Cooking the Chicken
    • Heat your wok or frying pan over high heat until it's very hot. Add the vegetable oil, swirling to coat the surface.
    • Add the shallots, garlic, and chilies to the wok. Stir-fry them for about 30 seconds or until aromatic, being careful not to burn the garlic.
    • Toss in the chicken. Let it sear for a minute before stirring, then continue to stir-fry until it's mostly cooked throughâ€”about 3 to 4 minutes.
  3. Adding the Flavors
    • Once the chicken is cooked, pour in the sauce mixture and add the bell pepper. Stir everything together and let it cook for another 1-2 minutes.
    • Add the chicken broth or water to keep the mixture saucy and to deglaze the wok or pan, scraping up any browned bits.
  4. The Basil Finale
    • When the sauce has slightly thickened, toss in the holy basil leaves and stir until they wilt and are fully integrated into the dish, which should take less than a minute.
  5. Serve it Up
    • Taste and adjust the seasonings if needed. Then, transfer your Pad Krapow Gai to a serving plate.
    • For an authentic Thai street food experience, serve your stir-fry over a bed of steamed jasmine rice. Top with a fried egg if you like, with the yolk still runny.

Enjoy your Pad Krapow Gai with a side of crisp cucumber slices or a light salad to cut through the heat and richness of the dish. A chilled glass of Thai iced tea would complement this meal and provide a sweet, creamy respite from the spiciness. With each bite, you'll find yourself sinking deeper into the flavors of Thailand, a journey from your very own kitchen to the heart of Southeast Asia.
